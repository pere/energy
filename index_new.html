Why?

There are over 650 million people in Africa who have no access to electricity; this is in sharp contrast to the lack of investments in energy infrastructure and the continent’s untapped renewable energy potential

What?

The PV-DESI index aims to identify the most favourable countries to invest in PV decentralised options (including financial, environmental, and political factors)

To whom?

Designed for private sector, international donors, governments, and philanthropic stakeholders who wants to invest in solar PV decentralised options.

How?

The index is composed by 52 indicators classified in 4 pillars that effects stakeholder decision-making on PV mini-grid investments: economic, social, financial and environmental considerations

The financial and political pilars are defined positively when the financial and political situations in a country are stable

The environmental pilar is positive when the environmental conditions are favourable to PV mini-grids implementation

The social pilar is considered positive when the impacts of investing in PV mini-grids results in higher impacts. Therefore, indirectly when social conditions in the country are lower, the “attractiveness” for the different stakeholders is higher because the impact of bringing electricity can cause higher social impacts.
